Which of the following is the balanced net-ionic equation for the reaction between \(\ce{Li(s)}\) and water?

Approach

Group 1 metals react violently with water. Group 1 metals readily donate their valence electron. When water self-ionizes, the hydroxide ion reacts with lithium.

$$ \ce{H2O(l) <=> H+(aq) + OH-(aq)} $$

Hydrogen gas forms in addition to lithium hydroxide.

$$ \ce{Li(s) + H+(aq) + OH-(aq) -> LiOH(aq) + H2(g)} $$ $$ \ce{Li(s) + H2O(l) -> LiOH(aq) + H2(g)} $$

We can balance this reaction with the following coefficients,

$$ \ce{2Li(s) + 2H2O(l) -> 2LiOH(aq) + H2(g)} $$

There are no spectator ions in this reaction. The net ionic equation will include all of the species. Lithium hydroxide will be written out as ions.

$$\ce{2Li(s) + 2H2O(l) -> 2Li^+(aq) + 2OH^-(aq) + H2(g)} $$

If we write water out as ions, the hydroxide terms would cancel out. This equation does not accurately represent the situation.

$$\ce{2Li(s) + 2H+(aq) -> 2Li^+(aq) + H2(g)} $$
